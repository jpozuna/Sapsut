# Sapsut
Husky Hunt App Companion

Problem Statement
Northeastern Husky Hunt is the Resident Student Association’s (RSA) premier annual event — a fast-paced, 24-hour scavenger hunt that takes teams of undergraduate students across Northeastern’s campus and throughout the city of Boston.

During the Hunt, teams race to solve puzzles, uncover hidden locations, collect unique items, and complete a variety of creative and physical challenges. 

Today, the event relies on manual grading: organizers individually review every photo submission and text answer, score them by hand, and update standings themselves. These leads to weeks of delay between the event and the reveal of scores and the awarding of prizes as there are 50 teams with up to 12 people in each team. This is a significant bottleneck as the delayed feedback for participants extinguishes the weight of the award ceremony, the organizers are pushed to their limits who already have a lot on their plate since its at the end of the semester when they have finals and projects and co-op and job search. this also leads to potential inconsistent scoring due to the natual variance of human judgement.

Sapsut solves this by bringing AI into the equation. Teams submit photos and text answers through a mobile app; Claude grades each submission instantly, assigning a score and confidence level. High-confidence results are approved automatically. Low-confidence or borderline submissions surface to organizers for a quick human review — keeping humans in control where it matters, without drowning them in routine work.
